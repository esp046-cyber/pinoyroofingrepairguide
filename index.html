Advanced Water Management Systems

Concept: Multi-Barrier Defense

Barrier 1: Surface Drainage (Primary)

  • Proper slope minimum 10 degrees
  • No flat spots where water pools
  • Clean gutters channel water away

Barrier 2: Overlaps & Seals (Secondary)

  • Correct overlap direction (opposite wind)
  • Sealant at all horizontal joints
  • Flashing at all penetrations

Barrier 3: Underlayment (Tertiary)

  • Waterproof membrane under metal
  • Catches any water that passes roofing
  • Channels to drainage points

Barrier 4: Ventilation (Backup)

  • Prevents condensation (looks like leak)
  • Removes moisture from attic space
  • Extends overall roof life by decades

Problem: Condensation vs. Actual Leak

How to Tell the Difference:

Signs of CONDENSATION:

  • Water appears even when not raining
  • More common in early morning
  • Affects large areas uniformly
  • Surface feels damp, not dripping wet
  • Worse when cooking/bathing without ventilation

Signs of ACTUAL LEAK:

  • Only occurs during/after rain
  • Specific location, not uniform
  • May have water stains/rings
  • Dripping or flowing water
  • Can trace path from ceiling to source

Condensation Solutions:

1 Install roof vents (2-4 vents minimum)

2 Add soffit vents for air intake

3 Install insulation with vapor barrier

4 Use exhaust fans when cooking

Cost: ‚Ç±3,000-10,000

Problem: Rust Spreading Despite Treatment

Root Cause Analysis:

Cause 1: Galvanic Corrosion (Different Metals Touching)

  • Example: Steel screws in aluminum sheets
  • Example: Copper flashing touching galvanized yero
  • Solution: Insert plastic washers between different metals
  • Prevention: Use compatible fasteners always

Cause 2: Coastal Environment (Salt Air)

  • Normal rust treatment insufficient
  • Requires marine-grade coatings
  • Solution: Zinc-rich primer + epoxy topcoat
  • Maintenance: Wash with freshwater quarterly

Cause 3: Poor Drainage (Standing Water)

  • Even treated rust returns if water sits
  • Solution: Improve roof slope or add drainage
  • Prevention: Never install flat metal roofs

Cause 4: Coating Failure (Paint Not Adhering)

  • Painted over oil, grease, or moisture
  • Solution: Strip all paint, clean thoroughly, re-prime
  • Prevention: Proper surface prep (80% of paint success)

Problem: Noise - Identifying and Fixing

Noise Type 1: Popping/Cracking (Thermal Expansion)

  • Cause: Metal expanding/contracting with temperature
  • Worse: Morning and evening (temp changes)
  • Solution: Use slotted holes for fasteners (allows movement)
  • Solution: Don't overtighten screws

Noise Type 2: Rumbling (Wind Vibration)

  • Cause: Large unsupported spans, loose fasteners
  • Worse: During windy conditions
  • Solution: Add more purlins (reduce span to 3ft max)
  • Solution: Install intermediate screws

Noise Type 3: Drumming (Rain Impact)

  • Cause: Thin gauge metal + no insulation
  • Worse: During heavy rain
  • Solution: Install acoustic insulation underneath
  • Solution: Add ceiling to create air gap

Noise Type 4: Scraping/Rubbing

  • Cause: Sheets rubbing against each other or branches
  • Worse: When windy
  • Solution: Trim nearby tree branches
  • Solution: Add rubber spacers between sheets

Diagnostic: Water Stain Pattern Analysis

Stain patterns tell the story:

Pattern A: Circular Ring Stains

  • Indicates: Slow, persistent leak
  • Likely source: Penetration (vent, pipe) not sealed
  • Look for: Flashing failure around objects

Pattern B: Linear Stains Following Beams

  • Indicates: Water running along structural member
  • Likely source: Leak is uphill from stain
  • Look for: Screw holes, seam failures along beam path

Pattern C: Large Area, Uniform Dampness

  • Indicates: Condensation, not leak
  • Likely source: Poor ventilation
  • Look for: Mold growth, musty smell

Pattern D: Stains Only at Edges/Walls

  • Indicates: Flashing failure
  • Likely source: Wall-roof junction not sealed
  • Look for: Gaps between wall and roofing

Tamang Pag-overlap ng Yero

  • Side overlap: 1 corrugation (mga 2 inches)
  • End overlap: 6 inches minimum
  • Low pitch roof: 8-12 inches overlap
  • Steep roof: 4-6 inches okay na

  • Laging gumamit ng rubber washer
  • Check if perpendicular ang screws

Professional Roof Painting Procedure

Surface Preparation (Most Important!):

1 Choose dry season (January-March best)

2 Sweep entire roof surface clean

3 Power wash on low setting (1000 PSI max)

4 Let dry completely (2-3 days)

5 Wire brush all rust spots aggressively

6 Apply rust converter on affected areas

7 Wait 24 hours for converter to work

8 Sand rough areas smooth (220-grit)

9 Wipe with degreaser solution

10 Let surface dry again

Priming Phase:

11 Stir primer thoroughly (5 minutes)

12 Pour into paint tray

13 Start from highest point (ridge)

14 Use long-nap roller for corrugated surface

15 Apply in direction of water flow (downward)

16 Get into all corrugation valleys

17 Use brush for edges and overlaps

18 Apply even coat - no thick spots

19 Let dry per manufacturer specs (usually 4-6 hours)

20 Inspect coverage in good light

First Paint Coat:

21 Stir paint thoroughly

22 Apply same direction as primer

23 Maintain wet edge to avoid lap marks

24 Work in sections (complete one before moving)

25 Check coverage from ground level

26 Let dry 24 hours minimum

Second Coat:

27 Light sand if any drips exist (400-grit)

28 Wipe dust with tack cloth

29 Apply second coat same technique

30 Focus on thin spots from first coat

31 Let cure 48 hours before rain exposure

Finishing Touches:

32 Touch up screw heads individually

33 Paint ridge caps if applicable

34 Apply third coat on high-wear areas (optional)

35 Final inspection from all angles

Time: 3-5 days | Cost: ‚Ç±8,000-25,000 (50 sqm roof)

Advanced Troubleshooting Guide

Problem: Leak but No Visible Damage

  • Check: Condensation from poor ventilation
  • Check: Capillary action under overlaps
  • Check: Wind-driven rain through vents
  • Solution: Thermal imaging camera to find exact path

Problem: Excessive Noise in Rain/Wind

  • Cause: Inadequate fasteners (vibration)
  • Cause: Large unsupported spans
  • Solution: Add more purlins (max 4ft spacing)
  • Solution: Install sound-deadening pads under screws

Problem: Rapid Rust Despite Treatment

  • Cause: Dissimilar metal contact (galvanic corrosion)
  • Cause: Salt air (coastal areas)
  • Solution: Use plastic washers to separate metals
  • Solution: Apply sacrificial zinc coating
